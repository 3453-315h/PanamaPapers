key findings

1

The pharmaceutical industry’s abuse of transfer pricing and thin capitalisation strategies illicitly inflates the research and development (R&D) costs of new medicines by an estimated 80%, pushing the cost of medicines out of the reach of millions of the world’s poorest communities.

2

Pharmaceutical industry’s financial model for medicine R&D and the ‘intangible capital’ that underpins the resulting IP is designed to actively prevent competitive innovation by locking knowledge into monopolies that ‘upstarts’ cannot penetrate. The result is millions of unnecessary deaths across the world.

3

In Africa, over 60% of the pharmaceutical industry’s global drug profits are generated largely as a result of the industry’s manipulation of the pricing system, aided by poor regulation against direct-to-consumer marketing and the dangerous use of ‘me too’ and ‘off label’ drug use prescribed by doctors.

4

Pharmaceutical industry’s R&D costs (estimated at $228bn/yr) are dwarfed by the industry’s spending on marketing and administration (estimated at $740bn/yr). .
